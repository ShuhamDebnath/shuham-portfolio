description

Information We Collect

The apps we build collect only anonymous, aggregate analytics — such as install counts, crash reports, and whether the app is being actively used — to help us fix bugs and improve the experience. We do not collect personal information, and any content you create or store within an app (notes, entries, logs, etc.) stays on your device and is never transmitted to us.

bar_chart

How Information Is Used

The anonymous analytics data described above is used solely to detect crashes, understand general usage patterns, and prioritise improvements to app stability and user experience. It is not used for advertising, profiling, or any purpose beyond making the apps work better.

group

Third-Party Services

Our apps use standard mobile analytics and crash-reporting tools (such as Android Vitals or Firebase Crashlytics) solely to collect the anonymous, aggregate data described above. These tools do not receive personal information, in-app content, or data entered by users. No advertising SDKs are used, no data is shared with advertising networks, and no data is sold to or shared with data brokers.

schedule

Data Retention

We retain your information only for as long as necessary to provide you with our services and as described in this policy. We may also retain this information to comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce agreements.

Your Choices

You can choose not to provide certain information, though it may limit your ability to use some features of our apps. You can also manage your communication preferences and opt-out of certain data collection through your device settings.

Children's Privacy

Our apps are not intended for children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we become aware that a child has provided us with personal information, we will take steps to delete it.

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new policy on this page and updating the 'Last Updated' date at the top.
